The effect of Dogecoin on the Shiba Inu Breed

Within our modern day society we now see currencies in various different forms. Throughout history we have seen currency evolve from livestock/spices to precious metals to the standard form of paper money. Just within the past couple of decades we are able to encapsulate our finances through the swipe of our card. However, now we are entering the age of a more digital currency that is better known as cryptos. These forms of currency are unique as they aren't tangible and are based on the community and trust within that to gauge the value of the currency. With the biggest crypto currency being Bitcoin which blazed the trail for its kind in the early 2000’s, we see that it's grown massively and one bitcoin is worth tens of thousands of dollars. What about the other crypto currencies? How are they doing? This is where Dogecoin comes in.

Dogecoin is a fairly new type of crypto and has taken the internet by storm with one of its main promoters and supporters being Elon Musk, the ceo of tesla, and a multi-billionaire. Everyone knows who Elon Musk is for his worldwide accomplishments and achievements to humanity technologically. Dogecoin isnt worth as much compared to bitcoin only sitting around a handful of cents per coin but the internet has deemed it the meme coin and is an ongoing joke within places such as Reddit and other forms of social media. Financial advisors would never tell you to dump money into this crypto but a lot of people still do just to support the joke/meme of Dogecoin.

Dogecoin has seen its normal hills and valleys of its price but over the span of its life it has grown extensively no thanks to the internet and Elon Musk himself to promote it. With the amount of exposure to the world there is no doubt that this crypto currency will be here to stick around as it will forever be symbolizing a meme currency.

Inadvertently, the face behind Dogecoin is the “doge” meme itself on the face of the coin. This is a picture of a shiba inu dog that is very popular in Japan. This is where I wanted to dive deeper into how the face of Dogecoin, a currency that is solely digital along with the meme, affects the breed of shiba inu’s. Over the lifespan of this meme and Dogecoin there has been an increasing amount of interest shown for this dog breed to the point where people no longer call them by their breed name but by “doge”.

According to slate.com, they talked with organizations that specialize in shiba inus and they have said that there are a lot more owners that will impulse buy these dogs for their cuteness and the fact that they are a meme without doing much research behind the dog breed itself. Apparently, these dogs are difficult to raise as they are a more primitive breed making it hard for the owner to control. The specialists warn that inexperienced owners are subject to thousands of dollars in repairs caused by their puppy without proper knowledge of these pets as the shiba inu are genetically closest to a wolf.
